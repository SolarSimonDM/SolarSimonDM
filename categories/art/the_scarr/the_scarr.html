THE S.C.A.R.R.

S.C.A.R.R. Character Descriptions

Archibald Harley / Archie

A jolly 93-years-young farmer from Kansas, with an unhealthy obsession affinity for strawberries. While his age marks him as rusty, he's as oiled as a leaking tanker. He hangs out with a pink elephant and a permanently drunk cyber pirate, whenever he doesn't practice his rap freestyles, slap bass, and strange kung fu moves.

Rob the Pink Elephant / Robert / Robbie / Roobie / Sir Robert Byron Edward Cedric Ogilvy Pinkskin the Pink Elephant

Rob is a pink elephant of class and short temper that hunts interdimensional rodents. More specifically, a highly-trained B.rutal A.nimalistic D.readed G.argantuan E.xtra-terrestrial R.ogue hunter, who has been Arch's companion for several years. He jumps between dimensions and always skips band practice on Fridays.

Captain Richie / Richard Davidson

The long-gone hero, Rich was trapped in an interdimensional hell, known as "Cyberspace". There, he fought off robotic robbers, termination thiefs and other machinery. He joined a group of space pirates and became their leader. Now that he's returned, he has been cursed with "constant pirate speak". Always drunk, he can never have enough of his great disgusting rum.

Tom Grooves

Tom Grooves is a phenomenal pianist, orchestrator, conductor of his own symphony orchestra, dancer, choreographer, host of his late-night talk show "The Too Late Night Show", and founder and owner of the prestigious "Tom's Music School". He's known for his two-finger walk to the stage, his rich hair, and his abysmally hard music arrangements. He lives in his own skyscraper in Groove City.

Rupus

Rupus is a depressed French (?) director that always ends his sentences with a period to signify his pain. Always the life of the party, he likes to hide inside his barrel for hours on end. Whenever he is not playing his accordion and shooting himself, he shoots S.C.A.R.R. episodes.

S.C.A.R.R. Notable Moments

Oakwood Ball

We were younglings, fools of our modern image. We were having a fun, yet competitive game of tag, which resulted in a tie between Reggie from Junior's and Margaret Thompson. After the game, I heard them talk about the annual Oakwood Ball. Then, I remembered that I was super into Teresa from the class down the hall. Of course, as a gentlephant of my age, I took the liberty to settle for someone who was actually in my league, like Martha.

Martha was polite yet adventurous, fun but smart, and all other contrasting positive features. After that mental argument between me and myself, I decided to talk about it to my best friend. The one who I would tell everything, no matter what. Sadly, I had no friend like that, so reached to you. We sat outside the cafeteria, which had salmon as the main dish of the day. You told me about that weird rash on your nose, but I told you it was nothing (turns out it was a massive Rogue infection, more on that next time), the one thing led to the other, and I told you about Martha. You, however, didn't have anyone in mind for the dance, but you were chill about it.

A day before the ball, and you still were partner-less, while I, gentlephant myself, already had asked Martha for the ball (it took about two gallons of eggnog to muster all that courage). I don't remember her response, but I believe it was something along the lines of "Urm... uhh... yeeaah. Okay, sure." I borrowed Frank the clown's car, rented a fancy suit, and grew some flowers in my back yard, only to give them to my future companion (and potential future wife). It was truly a dream come true for a young Roguehunter like yours truly.

30 minutes before the ball. The time is almost ready, my palms are sweaty, there's sweat on my tuxedo already. My mother had made tortellini that night, so I was a bit nauseous. But, I took Frank's car, drove it to university and made it to the ball! ...noooottt exactly. Frank's old car isn't supposed to support so heavy objects, even though it holds about twenty clowns every day. So, the poor pachyderm bathed in pink was stuck on the road for a solid sixty minutes, or most commonly known as "an hour".

When I make it to Oakwoods' squeaking front doors, an exhalation of breath comes out from my body. I rush in to the main room, to see what? My best chance to have children, hanging onto a tall, hunched weirdo with a weird infection on his nose. Then, everything stopped. I saw my own life flash before my trunk. I don't remember much after that, but I do remember having to soak three pillowcases that night. It truly was a night to remember. But not a night to relive.
I never had forgiven Arch for that unexpected turn of events, but I never had told him about it, and probably never will.

Yours truly,
Robert Edward Cedric Ogilvy the Pink Elephant
